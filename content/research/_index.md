A central theme of my research lies at the intersection of separation science, colloidal and interfacial phenomena, and functional nanomaterials. By understanding and manipulating nanoscale interactions at fluids and interfaces, we design high-efficiency systems for radionuclide and actinide separations, advanced water purification, and bioinspired engineering.

## 1. Chemical Speciation in Structured Fluids

<div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr; gap: 20px; margin-bottom: 25px;">
  <div style="text-align: center;">
    <img src="/images/nanoscale_toc.png" alt="Chemical Speciation Graphic" style="width: 100%; max-width: 500px; border-radius: 8px; border: 1px solid var(--border);">
    <p style="font-size: 0.85rem; color: var(--secondary); margin-top: 5px;">Figure 1: Small-angle X-ray scattering and spectroscopic study of extracted organic phases enable connecting molecular and nanoscale structural features with bulk extraction performance in complex fluids that extract metal ions</p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <p>This research direction focuses on understanding the molecular-scale organization, micellar aggregation, and phase boundaries of complex extractant fluids used in highly selective radionuclide, actinide, and lanthanide separations. By linking micro-scale speciation directly to macro-scale separation efficiency, we optimize extraction systems for extreme industrial conditions.</p>

   <p><b>Core Techniques:</b> Small-Angle X-ray Scattering (SAXS), Spectroscopy (FTIR, UV-vis, NMR, TRLIFS), Liquid-Liquid Extraction, and Thermodynamic Models.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## 2. Ions at Charged Interfaces

<div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr; gap: 20px; margin-bottom: 25px;">
  <div style="text-align: center;">
    <img src="/images/research/interfaces.jpg" alt="Ions at Interfaces Graphic" style="width: 100%; max-width: 500px; border-radius: 8px; border: 1px solid var(--border);">
    <p style="font-size: 0.85rem; color: var(--secondary); margin-top: 5px;">Figure 2: </p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <p>We investigate the fundamental thermodynamics and transport mechanics of selective ion binding at Langmuir monolayers of extractant-like surfactants. Particular emphasis is on the adsorption of multivalent ions including lanthanides and the role of interfacial hydraiton on charge transport across membranes and fluid interfaces.</p>

   <p><b>Core Techniques:</b> Grazing-Incidence Diffraction (GID), X-ray reflectivity (XRR), X-ray fluorescence near total reflection (XFNTR), Langmuir compression isotherms, and vibrational sum frequency generation spectroscopy (VSFG).</p>
  </div>
</div>

<details>
<summary><b> Click to view past projects and highlights</b></summary>

### Projects:
* Trivalent lanthanide ions at negatively charged Lngmuir monolayers 
* Ion-specificity in the interfacial structure of cationic Langmuir monolayers
* Adsorption of anionic complexes of Pt-group metals at cationic Langmuir monolayers

### Methodology Highlight:
By employing surface-sensitive X-ray scattering and vibrational specotrscopic techniques under dynamic grazing conditions, we characterize the floating monolayers in situ to capture real-time interfacial composition and structure.

## 3. Nanoparticle Synthesis & Self-Assembly

<div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr; gap: 20px; margin-bottom: 25px;">
  <div style="text-align: center;">
    <img src="/images/research/nanoparticles.jpg" alt="Nanoparticle Assembly Graphic" style="width: 100%; max-width: 500px; border-radius: 8px; border: 1px solid var(--border);">
    <p style="font-size: 0.85rem; color: var(--secondary); margin-top: 5px;">Figure 3: 2-, and 3-d superlattices of gold nanoparticles obtained by inter-polymer complexation-driven self-assembly.</p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <p>This thrust explores the controlled colloidal synthesis and directed macroscopic self-assembly of functional nanoparticles into organized, highly periodic superlattices. By tuning inter-particle forces and surface ligands, we manufacture materials with unique optical, magnetic, and catalytic properties.</p>

   <p><b>Core Techniques:</b> Colloidal Wet-Chemical Synthesis, Nanoparticle Surface Functionalization, Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS), Small-angle X-ray scattering (SAXS), Grazing-incidence SAXS, X-ray reflectivity, Gibbs monolayers, Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM).</p>
  </div>
</div>
