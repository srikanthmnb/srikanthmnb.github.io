I am a chemical scientist and researcher specializing in separation science, colloidal and interfacial phenomena, and functional nanomaterials. My research focuses on elucidating the fundamental physical chemistry, molecular mechanisms, and mesoscale structures that drive selective separations, self-assembly, and coordination chemistry in complex fluid environments.

My core expertise lies in utilizing high-brilliance synchrotron X-ray scattering and spectroscopy tools—including SAXS/WAXS, GISAXS, and X-ray reflectometry (XRR)—to map complex fluids and structural interfaces at the nanoscale. By combining advanced material characterization with surface science, automated analytical instrumentation, and computational data analysis, my work bridges fundamental molecular phenomena with predictable chemical engineering outcomes.

Beyond research execution, I am deeply committed to professional service, serving as a peer reviewer for high-impact journals and the U.S. Department of Energy, and mentoring the next generation of scientists and undergraduate engineers.

## Professional Experience

### Assistant Chemist | Argonne National Laboratory (2021 — 2025)
*Heavy Elements and Separation Science Group, Lemont, IL*
* Investigated the mesoscale structure and solution speciation of complex fluids containing tertiary amines and ammonium-based organic cations using SAXS and spectroscopy.
* Conceptualized and executed separation chemistry studies of lanthanides and actinides in complex fluid environments, directly supporting the selective extraction of critical f-elements and radionuclides.
* Synthesized Ce(IV) and Pu(IV) oxide nanoparticles, establishing the foundational links between their surface chemistry, morphology, and phase behavior in concentrated electrolytes.

### Postdoctoral Appointee | Argonne National Laboratory (2019 — 2021)
*Heavy Element Chemistry and Separation Science Group, Lemont, IL (Supervisor: Dr. Ahmet Uysal)*
* Investigated ion adsorption at charged 2D Langmuir monolayers as model systems for selective industrial separations.
* Discovered the spontaneous formation of inverted bilayers at air/aqueous interfaces and mapped the essential role of interfacial ion-hydration in solvent extraction networks.

### Graduate Research Assistant | Iowa State University (2013 — 2018)
*Dept. of Chemical Engineering, Ames, IA (Advisor: Dr. Surya Mallapragada)*
* Pioneered hierarchical self-assembly protocols for functional hybrid materials and highly ordered nanoparticle superlattices using interpolymer complexation.
